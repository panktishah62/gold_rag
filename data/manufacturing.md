# Gold Manufacturing Guide

## Karat Differences

- 24K gold contains 99.9% pure gold.
- 22K gold contains 91.6% pure gold.
- 18K gold contains 75% pure gold.
- 14K gold contains 58.5% pure gold.

Higher karat means higher gold purity but lower durability.
Lower karat gold is stronger and more suitable for intricate jewelry designs.

---

## Common Manufacturing Methods

### Casting
Casting involves melting gold and pouring it into molds.
It is commonly used for mass production.

Advantages:
- Suitable for complex designs
- Efficient for large batches

Disadvantages:
- Higher wastage compared to handmade processes

---

### Handmade Jewelry
Jewelry is crafted manually by skilled artisans.

Advantages:
- Higher craftsmanship
- Lower porosity
- Premium positioning

Disadvantages:
- Higher labor cost
- Slower production

---

## Wastage in Gold Manufacturing

Wastage refers to gold lost during:

- Melting
- Filing
- Polishing
- Casting sprues removal

Typical wastage ranges:
- Casting: 5% to 12%
- Handmade: 2% to 6%

Wastage percentage directly impacts final pricing.

---

## Making Charges

Making charges are labor costs involved in manufacturing jewelry.

They can be calculated:
- As a percentage of gold value (e.g., 8%–15%)
- Or as fixed cost per gram

Higher complexity designs attract higher making charges.

---

## Hallmarking

Hallmarking certifies gold purity.

Common marks:
- 22K916 (91.6% purity)
- 18K750 (75% purity)

Hallmarking increases customer trust and resale value.
